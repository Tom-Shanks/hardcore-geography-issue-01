About the Project

Hardcore Geography is a multi-format research project documenting the systematic displacement of Denver's DIY music venues through geographic analysis, audio archaeology, and community resistance narratives.

The project combines academic research with hardcore aesthetic to examine how municipal zoning codes, real estate speculation, and planning policy collaborate to eliminate spaces of cultural resistance. Through essays, interactive mapping, audio documentation, and print publication, we trace the sonic archaeology of gentrification and document strategies of underground persistence.

Methodology: Spatial analysis of 30+ venues (1995-2025), community interviews, ambient audio recording, historical flyer documentation, and policy analysis of zoning changes and development patterns.

Outputs: Three academic essays (5,099 words), interactive venue map, three-episode podcast series, 32-page print zine, and open data package for community use and further research.
